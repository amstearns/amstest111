# Technology Assessment: Renewable Energy for Mid-Size Construction Firms

*Summary of detailed research from `output/internal/research/electric-heavy-equipment.md`, `output/internal/research/onsite-solar-power.md`, and `output/internal/research/green-building-systems.md`*

## 1. Electric Heavy Equipment

- **Market:** $14.50B (2024), projected $29.00B by 2029 (20.7% CAGR)
- **Top models:** Volvo EC230 Electric (450 kWh, 7–8 hr), Komatsu PC210E (up to 8 hr), Cat 320 Electric, Volvo L120 Electric (282 kWh, 5–9 hr), Liebherr LTC 1050-3.1E (50T crane)
- **Purchase premium:** 40–100% over diesel equivalents
- **10-year TCO (20T excavator):** Near parity — $646,600.00 (electric) vs. $651,500.00 (diesel) without incentives
- **Charging infrastructure:** $82,000.00–$353,000.00 for DC fast charging; portable solutions available
- **Recommendation:** Adopt for urban/noise-restricted/indoor sites and compact equipment; wait for mid-size

## 2. On-Site Solar Power

- **Solar+storage cost per kWh:** $0.04–$0.09 (amortized) vs. diesel $0.31–$0.58
- **Break-even:** 21–78 months depending on geography and incentives
- **Best entry point:** Hybrid solar-diesel reduces diesel runtime 60–83%, fuel costs 40–60%
- **Key systems:** Voltstack, BoxPower, Sunboxt, POWR2, Sesame Solar
- **LFP batteries dominate:** >80% of new commercial ESS; 6,000–8,000 cycle life
