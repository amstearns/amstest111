# Competitive Case Study Data: Mid-Size Construction Firms with Renewable Energy Strategies

## Firm Selection Criteria
- Revenue range: $150M–$450M annually
- Employees: 200–450 staff
- Geography: North American operations
- Renewable energy adoption: 3+ years operational experience
- Proven track record: Measurable financial and operational impact

## Case Study 1: TerraCore Construction Inc. (Fictional - Based on Real Composite Data)

### Company Overview
- **Headquarters**: Dallas, Texas
- **Founded**: 1998
- **Annual Revenue**: $287.3M
- **Employees**: 342
- **Specialization**: Commercial & mixed-use development
- **Market Presence**: Texas, Oklahoma, New Mexico, Colorado
- **Green Certifications**: LEED Accredited, Green Business Bureau Certified

### Renewable Energy Strategy
- **Entry Point**: 2020—solar installation on corporate HQ
- **Strategic Goal**: Differentiate in commercial market; reduce operating costs
- **Market Positioning**: "Solar-First Contractor"—lead with renewable solutions in RFPs
- **Timeline**: Phased 5-year rollout across operations and projects

### Implementation Details
- **Initial Investment**: $450,000 for corporate solar (50 kW)
- **Training Program**: Certified 18 project managers in solar project delivery (2021)
- **Supply Chain**: Partnerships with 2 solar installers and 1 battery storage vendor
- **Project Integration**: Renewable systems in 23 commercial projects (2021–2024)
- **Workforce**: Dedicated 4-person renewable energy division by 2023

### Results (2020–2024)
- **Corporate Energy Savings**: $47,200.00 annually
- **Payback Period**: 9.5 years
- **Projects with Renewable Component**: 23 (23% of commercial portfolio)
- **Green Premium Captured**: Average 4.1% contract uplift
- **Revenue from Renewable Services**: $3.8M annually (by 2024)
- **Client Retention**: 18% improvement in repeat business from solar-equipped projects
- **Bid Win Rate**: 34% → 52% in renewable-eligible projects

### Lessons Learned
1. **Workforce Development Critical**: Early investment in training paid off via faster project delivery
2. **Supply Chain Lock-In**: Long-term partnerships reduced procurement time by 40%
3. **Client Education**: Projects with detailed pre-bid renewable analysis won 28% more frequently
4. **Financing Innovation**: In-house solar financing (PACE partnerships) was key differentiator
5. **Market Timing**: First-mover advantage in Texas market lasted ~2.5 years before competition emerged
