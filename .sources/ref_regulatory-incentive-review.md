## 1. Inflation Reduction Act (IRA) - Construction-Relevant Provisions

The Inflation Reduction Act (signed August 16, 2022) represented the most significant clean energy legislation in U.S. history, allocating approximately $369 billion toward energy security and climate change programs. However, the One Big Beautiful Bill Act (OBBBA), signed July 4, 2025, has significantly modified, curtailed, or repealed many of these provisions. The table below reflects the original IRA provisions alongside their current post-OBBBA status.

### 1.1 Investment Tax Credit (ITC) - Section 48 / Section 48E

| Parameter | Original IRA Value | Post-OBBBA Status |
|---|---|---|
| Base ITC rate | 6.0% of qualified investment | Solar/wind: phased out by Dec 31, 2027 |
| Enhanced ITC rate (with PWA compliance) | 30.0% of qualified investment | Remains for projects begun before phase-out |
| Domestic content bonus | +10.0% additional credit | Tightened domestic content requirements |
| Energy community bonus | +10.0% additional credit | Retained with modifications |
| Maximum possible ITC | 50.0% (30% base + 10% domestic + 10% energy community) | Reduced windows |
| Eligible technologies | Solar, geothermal, battery storage, microgrid controllers, biomass | Solar/wind must be placed in service before Jan 1, 2028 |
| Geothermal projects | Begin construction before Jan 1, 2035 | Retained |
| Original sunset | Phase-out tied to emissions reduction targets or 2032-2034 | Accelerated; solar/wind phase-out begins 2028 |

**Confidence Level:** HIGH - sourced from IRS guidance, IRA Tracker (Columbia/EDF), Grant Thornton analysis

**Key Detail:** The ITC under Section 48E provides a technology-neutral clean electricity investment credit. The OBBBA retains the core structure but accelerates the phase-out for solar and wind specifically: facilities must be placed in service before December 31, 2027, unless construction begins before July 5, 2026.

**Prevailing Wage and Apprenticeship (PWA) Requirements:**
- Must pay prevailing wages during construction and for the first 5 years of operation
- Must employ qualified apprentices for at least 12.5% of labor hours (15.0% for projects beginning construction after 2023)
- Without PWA compliance, the credit drops from 30.0% to 6.0% -- an 80.0% reduction
- Correction and penalty mechanisms exist for good-faith compliance failures

**Sources:**
- IRS Form 3468 Instructions (2024): https://irs.gov/pub/irs-pdf/i3468.pdf
- IRA Tracker - Section 13102: https://iratracker.org/programs/ira-section-13102-renewable-energy-investment-tax-credit/
- Grant Thornton OBBBA Analysis (Sep 2025): https://www.grantthornton.com/insights/alerts/tax/2025/insights/energy-incentives-under-obbba-what-you-need-to-know

| Parameter | Value | Confidence |

**Critical Note for Construction Firms:** The Section 45W credit is **no longer available** as of September 30, 2025. Construction firms that did not acquire electric heavy equipment (e.g., electric excavators, wheel loaders) before this date cannot claim this credit. The IRS confirmed that vehicles must have been "acquired" by September 30, 2025, with a binding written contract and either a nominal down payment or trade-in sufficient to establish acquisition.

**Estimated total program value:** The Joint Committee on Taxation estimated $14.40 billion in Commercial Clean Vehicle Credits would be claimed by 2028.

### 1.5 Energy Efficient Commercial Building Deduction - Section 179D

| Parameter | 2025 Value | 2026 Value | Confidence |
|---|---|---|---|
| Base deduction (min at 25% energy reduction) | $0.58/sq ft | $0.59/sq ft | HIGH |
| Base deduction (max, ~50% energy reduction) | $1.16/sq ft | $1.19/sq ft | HIGH |
| Enhanced deduction with PWA (min) | $2.90/sq ft | $2.97/sq ft | HIGH |
| Enhanced deduction with PWA (max) | $5.80/sq ft | $5.94/sq ft | HIGH |
| Base increment per % point over 25% | $0.02/sq ft | $0.02/sq ft | HIGH |
| Enhanced increment per % point over 25% | $0.12/sq ft | $0.12/sq ft | HIGH |
| Energy reduction threshold | ≥ 25.0% vs. ASHRAE 90.1 reference | Same | HIGH |
| **POST-OBBBA STATUS** | **TERMINATES for construction beginning after June 30, 2026** | | HIGH |

**Construction Firm Relevance:** Section 179D is particularly important for construction firms because of the "designer allocation" provision. When buildings are owned by tax-exempt entities (government agencies, nonprofits, schools, hospitals), the owner can allocate the deduction to the designer primarily responsible for the energy-efficient specifications. Design-build contractors often qualify.

**Example Impact (ESTIMATE):** A 50,000 sq ft commercial building achieving 50%+ energy cost reduction with PWA compliance:
- Maximum deduction: 50,000 x $5.94 = **$297,000.00** (2026 rates)
- Without PWA: 50,000 x $1.19 = **$59,500.00**

## 2. One Big Beautiful Bill Act (OBBBA) - Critical Updates

Signed into law July 4, 2025, the OBBBA represents the most significant rollback of clean energy incentives since the IRA was enacted. The following table summarizes key termination dates critical for construction firms:

### Summary of OBBBA Termination/Phase-out Dates

| Credit/Deduction | Section | Termination/Phase-out Date | Trigger |
|---|---|---|---|
| Commercial Clean Vehicle Credit | 45W | **September 30, 2025** | Vehicles acquired after this date |
| New Clean Vehicle Credit | 30D | **September 30, 2025** | Vehicles acquired after this date |
| Previously Owned Clean Vehicle Credit | 25E | **September 30, 2025** | Vehicles acquired after this date |
| Residential Clean Energy Credit | 25D | **December 31, 2025** | Expenditures after this date |
| Energy Efficient Home Improvement Credit | 25C | **December 31, 2025** | Property placed in service after this date |
| Alt. Fuel Refueling Property Credit | 30C | **June 30, 2026** | Property placed in service after this date |
| New Energy Efficient Home Credit | 45L | **June 30, 2026** | Homes acquired after this date |
| 179D Building Deduction | 179D | **June 30, 2026** | Property beginning construction after this date |
| Clean Electricity ITC (solar/wind) | 48E | **December 31, 2027** | Must be placed in service by this date (if construction begins after Jul 5, 2026) |
| Clean Electricity PTC (solar/wind) | 45Y | **December 31, 2027** | Same as 48E for solar/wind |
| Clean Electricity ITC/PTC (other tech) | 48E/45Y | **2034-2036 phase-out** | Two-year phase-out beginning 2034 |

### New Foreign Entity Restrictions (OBBBA)

The OBBBA introduces significant restrictions on foreign involvement in clean energy projects:
- **Specified Foreign Entities (SFEs):** Entities from China, Russia, Iran, North Korea are ineligible
- **Foreign-Influenced Entities (FIEs):** Entities with significant SFE ownership (≥25.0% single entity, ≥40.0% aggregate) or ≥15.0% of debt from SFEs are ineligible
- **Material Assistance:** Projects receiving material assistance from prohibited foreign entities may lose credit eligibility
- **Effective Date:** Applies to most credits; Treasury must issue guidance by December 31, 2026

**Confidence Level:** HIGH
**Source:** Grant Thornton OBBBA Analysis; Arnold & Porter Advisory (Jul 2025): https://www.arnoldporter.com/en/perspectives/advisories/2025/07/from-ira-to-obbba-a-new-era-for-clean-energy-tax-credits

## 7. Key Takeaways for Construction Firms

### 7.1 Immediate Action Items (2026)

1. **Section 179D Window Closing:** Construction must BEGIN by June 30, 2026 for any projects to qualify. Accelerate design and procurement on energy-efficient commercial projects, particularly government and institutional buildings where the designer allocation applies.

2. **Section 30C (EV Charging) Deadline:** Property must be placed in service by June 30, 2026. If planning to install job-site EV charging infrastructure, act immediately.

3. **Solar/Wind ITC (48E) Construction Start:** For projects where construction begins after July 5, 2026, the facility must be placed in service by December 31, 2027. Ensure physical work of a significant nature begins before the deadline (the Five Percent Safe Harbor has been eliminated for projects beginning after September 2, 2025).

4. **Document PWA Compliance:** All remaining federal credits with the enhanced rate require prevailing wage and apprenticeship compliance. Ensure labor practices meet these requirements from day one.

### 7.2 Medium-Term Strategic Considerations (2026-2030)

1. **Carbon Pricing Creates Growing Advantage:** In Canada, carbon prices rising to $170.00 CAD/tonne by 2030 make clean energy equipment increasingly cost-competitive vs. diesel. Plan fleet transition accordingly.

2. **Building Performance Standards Expanding:** NYC LL97, Boston BERDO, Seattle BEPS, and others create guaranteed demand for energy-efficient construction and retrofit services. Position the firm as an expert in compliance-driven construction.

3. **Green Certification as Competitive Advantage:** With federal incentives shrinking, differentiation through LEED/WELL/Passive House expertise becomes more valuable as a market positioning strategy rather than a tax optimization strategy.

4. **Supply Chain Compliance:** New foreign entity restrictions under OBBBA affect equipment sourcing. Verify that solar panels, batteries, and other components meet domestic content requirements and are not manufactured by or for Specified Foreign Entities.
