# Financial Analysis Sections: Renewable Energy & Green Building ROI

**Disclaimer on Financial Projections**: All financial projections in this document are estimates based on 2025-2026 baseline costs and energy prices. Actual returns will vary based on project-specific factors including regional energy rates, equipment depreciation patterns, system utilization, and incentive program changes. Step-by-step calculations are provided to allow readers to adjust assumptions for their specific situations. Projections are clearly labeled as estimates and subject to a ±15% variance band. Readers should consult with financial advisors and conduct site-specific engineering studies before making capital allocation decisions.

## Executive Summary: Investment Attractiveness

For a mid-size construction firm (50-200 employees, $25M-$100M annual revenue), renewable energy and green building technology investments present compelling financial opportunities:

- **Electric Equipment**: 6-8 year full payback; 12-15% IRR over 10 years
- **On-Site Solar**: 4-5 year payback with incentives; 15-18% IRR  
- **Green Building Systems**: 4-7 year payback (phased implementation); 10-14% IRR

Cumulative portfolio approach (deploying all three technology categories) achieves:
- **5-year cumulative cash benefit**: $400K-$1.2M (for typical mid-size firm)
- **10-year NPV at 8% discount rate**: $800K-$2.5M (net present value after all costs)
- **Payback period**: 4-5 years on blended portfolio

**Key Insight**: The financial case is strongest for firms with 10+ year asset holding horizons and access to low-cost financing (PACE loans, green bonds). Firms with shorter project cycles or capital constraints should prioritize solar and building systems (faster payback) over long-term equipment investments.

## Part 5: Risk Assessment & Sensitivity

### 5.1 Downside Scenarios

**What if electricity prices don't escalate as expected?**

| Scenario | Electricity Inflation Rate | 10-Year NPV Impact | Risk Level |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Base case** | 3.0%/year | $320,000 | Baseline |
| **Low inflation** | 1.5%/year (weak demand) | +$140,000 | Moderate risk |
| **No inflation** | 0%/year (oversupply) | -$80,000 | High risk |
| **Deflation** | -1%/year (rare scenario) | -$220,000 | Extreme risk |

**Mitigation**: Power Purchase Agreements (PPAs) with utilities can lock in long-term rates if desired (reduces optionality but provides certainty).

**What if incentive programs expire early?**

| Scenario | ITC Level | PACE Availability | 10-Year NPV Impact | Likelihood |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Base case** | 30% through 2032 | Maintained | $320,000 | 60% |
| **Early ITC drop** | Drops to 22% in 2028 | Maintained | $220,000 | 25% |
| **PACE restrictions** | 30% maintained | Limited access | $180,000 | 10% |
| **Both compress** | 22% + limited PACE | Phased out | $85,000 | 5% |

**Mitigation**: Accelerate solar deployment to 2025-2026 window (maximizes ITC before decline). For electric equipment, phase purchases across 2025-2032 (preserves 30% credit throughout).

**What if key equipment vendors exit the market?**

| Risk | Impact | Probability | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Supply chain disruption | 6-12 month delays; 10-15% price inflation | 15% | Order 12 months in advance; diversify vendors |
| Warranty non-service (vendor bankruptcy) | Loss of free maintenance; battery replacement voids | 5% | Verify vendor stability; consider extended warranty |
| Technology leapfrog (newer tech obsolescence) | Current equipment becomes uncompetitive | 10% (within 10 years) | Plan for 2-3 generation cycles; retain flexibility |
