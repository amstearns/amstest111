## 2. Geothermal Systems

### 2.1 Ground-Source Heat Pump (GSHP) Technology Overview

Geothermal/ground-source heat pumps transfer heat between buildings and the earth, leveraging stable underground temperatures (50–60°F in most North American locations) for both heating and cooling. The system consists of:

1. **Ground Loop** — buried piping (vertical wells, horizontal trenches, or pond/lake loops) carrying heat-transfer fluid
2. **Heat Pump Unit** — indoor unit that exchanges heat with the loop
3. **Distribution System** — ductwork or hydronic system
4. **Controls** — thermostats, zoning, and BMS integration

**Performance:** COP (Coefficient of Performance) of 3.5–5.0, meaning for every unit of electricity consumed, 3.5–5.0 units of heating/cooling are delivered. This represents 50.0%–70.0% less electricity use than electric resistance heating and 30.0%–50.0% less than modern air-source heat pumps at design conditions.

**Confidence Level: HIGH** — COP ranges confirmed by DOE, IGSHPA, and manufacturer data.

### 2.2 Loop System Configurations

| Loop Type | Typical Application | Land Requirement | Relative Cost | Installation Depth/Length |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Vertical Closed-Loop** | Urban/suburban, small lots | Minimal | HIGH | 150–400 ft wells |
| **Horizontal Closed-Loop** | Rural, large lots | Large area | MEDIUM | 4–8 ft deep, 400–600 ft trenches |
| **Pond/Lake Loop** | Properties with water body | Existing water | LOW | Submerged in pond/lake |
| **Open-Loop (Well-to-Well)** | Ample groundwater | Moderate | MEDIUM | Depends on water table |

### 4.7 Smart Building ROI Reality Check

**Realistic Expectations (from industry analysis):**
- Energy savings: **15.0%–25.0%** (not the marketed 40%+)
- Payback period: **3–5 years** for phased implementations focused on HVAC and lighting
- Full smart building ROI: **5–8 years** for comprehensive Level 3 implementations
- Claims of 12–18 month payback are typically cherry-picked examples

**Recommended Phased Approach:**
1. **Phase 1 (Year 1):** HVAC optimization + basic monitoring = 50% of total savings potential; Investment: $300,000.00–$500,000.00 for 100k ft²
2. **Phase 2 (Year 2):** Lighting control + fault detection = 25% of savings; Investment: $200,000.00–$350,000.00
3. **Phase 3 (Year 3):** Predictive maintenance + advanced features = 15% of savings; Investment: $150,000.00–$250,000.00

**80/20 Rule:** Focus on HVAC + lighting first — delivers 80.0% of potential savings with 40.0% of complexity.
